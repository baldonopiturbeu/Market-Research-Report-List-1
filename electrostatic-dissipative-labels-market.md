<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electrostatic dissipative labels are categorized into two types based on their level of conductivity and application. Type I labels are designed for general use in environments with low levels of static, providing moderate electrostatic discharge (ESD) protection. They are suitable for everyday products. Type II labels, however, offer higher conductivity and are used in more critical applications, such as high-tech manufacturing and sensitive electronic components, where stronger ESD protection is essential. Both types help prevent damage from static electricity.</p></p>
