<p><p>The Parkinson's Disease drugs market includes several key types. Dopamine agonists mimic dopamine effects, alleviating motor symptoms. Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) prevent dopamine breakdown, enhancing its availability. Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors are primarily used in dementia-related symptoms by increasing acetylcholine levels. Glutamate inhibitors target excessive glutamate activity, potentially reducing neurodegeneration. Each category plays a vital role in symptom management and overall patient quality of life, addressing various aspects of Parkinson's Disease and contributing to comprehensive treatment strategies.</p></p>
