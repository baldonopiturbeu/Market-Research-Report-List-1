<p><p>Long Fiber Reinforced Thermoplastic (LFT) materials are categorized into two main types: LFT-G (Glass Fiber Reinforced) and LFT-D (Natural Fiber Reinforced). LFT-G utilizes glass fibers for enhanced strength, making it suitable for high-performance applications in automotive and aerospace industries where durability is critical. In contrast, LFT-D employs natural fibers like jute or flax, offering sustainable options with lower weight and environmental impact, ideal for sectors seeking eco-friendly materials. Both types leverage long fibers to improve mechanical properties and processing efficiency.</p></p>
